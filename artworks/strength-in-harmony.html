The Work, in Full
Concept

Two, and Inseparable

The piece resists a single reading. Two figures, two emotional temperatures, two veneer palettes — and yet the composition insists they cannot be separated. Harmony here is not the absence of tension. It is tension held with enough care that it becomes something else entirely.

Process

540 Pieces of Contrast

Both figures share the same panel surface but were constructed in opposing directions — lighter values in the left figure give way to deeper ones on the right, with the boundary between them resolved through a narrow band of transitional maple. The cutting sequence took three passes to balance. Some seams remain visible under raking light. That is intentional.

Reflection

 

“Harmony does not require sameness. These two are distinct and inseparable in the same breath.”
